### [![Image 1: (up)](http://primes.utm.edu/top20/includes/gifs/up3.gif)](http://primes.utm.edu/top20/page.php?id=3#top) Definitions and Notes

At this site we maintain a list of the 5000 largest known [primes](http://primes.utm.edu/glossary/xpage/Prime.html) (plus selected smller primes). It is [easy to show](http://primes.utm.edu/notes/proofs/) that there are infinitely many primes (in fact there are approximately _x_/ln(_x_) primes less than _x_, see "[How Many](http://primes.utm.edu/howmany.shtml)"). This means that each of the primes below are probably separated by many millions of other (curently unkown) primes.

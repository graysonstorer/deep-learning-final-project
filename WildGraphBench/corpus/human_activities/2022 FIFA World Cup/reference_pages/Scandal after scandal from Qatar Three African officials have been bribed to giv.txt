Scandal after scandal from Qatar: Three African officials have been bribed to give the vote to the Arab world for the organization of the World Cup
===================================================================================================================================================

![Image 5: 2022/11/qatar.png](https://telegrafi.com/media-library/2022-11-qatar-png.png?id=58070651&width=1245&height=700&quality=90&coordinates=0%2C0%2C1%2C0)

**A Qatari World Cup host has claimed that three African officials were offered bribes to support their bid to host the event.**

The Sun reports that an official made the allegations in the explosive Netflix series FIFA Uncovered.

* * *

Phaedra Almajid, who was in charge of the international press surrounding the bid, claims that Cameroon's Issa Hayatou, Ivory Coast's Jacques Anouma and Nigeria's Amos Adamu were offered €2.3m for their vote.

![Image 6: a88ea842 0001 0004 0000 000000780266 w1280 r1.77 fpx43.91 fpy50](blob:http://localhost/a734e144b315ae1f9491d3e7ed3282d6) Phaedra Al-Majid

Qatar was competing with Australia, Japan, South Korea and the USA for their bid for the 2022 World Cup.

Almajid claims that Hassan Al Thawadi, who led the bid, offered a serious sum to African football chiefs to secure their vote.

She told the documentary, via The Times: "We were talking about how Africa had been given the chance to host the World Cup and how the Arab world should be given its chance, and then Hassan offered Hayatou €1 million for Hayatou's (Cameroon) football federation and in return we wanted his vote."

"I remember that I just laughed and then he said that this was not enough, and so the price was raised to 2.3 million euros".

![Image 7: Qatar 2022 2](blob:http://localhost/1763867d8d7cb44e108ee4820e14218c)

"It was so simple: 'We will give you this money for your football federation, you give us your vote and we thank you very much.'"

"One by one we did the same with Anouma and Adamu. €2.3 million was offered to each member that evening in exchange for their vote. It was verbal, I never saw money".

It claims the offer came at a meeting of African football federations in January 2010.

However, she points out that the proposed offer is made to federations and not individuals.

She added: "It became very clear that the money was going to football, it was never said that it's going to go into your pocket, I have to stress that. I have no idea where they ended up."

"Before I went back to my hotel room, Hassan said to me, 'You never have to tell anyone about this again,' and I said okay."

Almajid had previously anonymously disclosed the offers to the Sunday Times after being sacked, however, later retracted her claims after being threatened by Qatar.

She explained in the documentary: "I'm starting to get threats, anonymous phone calls, emails, social media. They basically told me either you sign an affidavit stating that you lied or else we're going to prosecute you."

Al Thawadi has denied the claims, according to The Sun.

"My reaction, especially to the Phaedra situation, is disappointment. They are fundamentally false and there are facts on the ground that prove they are false," he said. /_**Telegraph**_/

[World Cup](https://www.independent.co.uk/topic/world-cup-2022)federations have been told by [Fifa](https://www.independent.co.uk/topic/fifa) that the rainbow flag will “not be prohibited” at stadiums for the next round of games, and that [Qatar](https://www.independent.co.uk/topic/qatar) have now given the governing body assurances on the issue after a[series of incidents](https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/qatar-one-love-armband-alex-scott-roy-keane-b2232223.html) that culminated in the team captains of seven Uefa federations [not wearing the OneLove armband.](https://www.independent.co.uk/tv/sport/world-cup-england-wales-armband-b2229679.html "World Cup: Why England and Wales will not wear OneLove armband")

The colours have become one of the [tense moments of the tournament](https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/japan-world-cup-fans-clean-stadium-b2232240.html), as so many players, officials and fans want to make a gesture of solidarity towards the [LGBTQ](https://www.independent.co.uk/topic/lgbtq-0)+ community over Qatar’s laws and culture around homosexuality.

Some supporters and media [have had items of clothing with rainbow flags confiscated,](https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/wales-usa-journalist-rainbow-qatar-fifa-b2229840.html "‘It’s not allowed’: US journalist ‘detained’ entering World Cup match for wearing rainbow t-shirt") most notably with security even instructing that [bucket hats should be removed](https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football/world-cup/wales-rainbow-bucket-hats-qatar-lgbtq-b2230495.html "Wales ‘extremely disappointed’ as fans ‘forced’ to remove rainbow bucket hats at World Cup").

_The Independent_ has been told of cases where Welsh fans were simply wearing the colours of the country – red, yellow, white and green – only to be told to take them off.

A number of federations have raised this with Fifa, given they were told beforehand that it would be an open World Cup, and the global governing body has now sent a note to federations that it has been given assurances by the tournament’s Safety and Security Operations Committee that rainbow items will no longer be confiscated.

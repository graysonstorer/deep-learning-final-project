During 1942 and 1943, the Russian city of Volgograd saw some of the heaviest fighting on World War II's Eastern Front. It has been described as a [crucial turning point](http://voiceofrussia.com/2010/02/02/4094008/) in the war, ultimately destroying Nazi Germany's 6th Army, but leaving the city itself ruined and causing the death of as many as a million soldiers.

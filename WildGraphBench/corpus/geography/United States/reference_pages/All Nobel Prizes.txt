All Nobel Prizes
================

Between 1901 and 2025, the Nobel Prizes and the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel were awarded 633 times to 1,026 people and organisations. With some receiving the Nobel Prize more than once, this makes a total of 990 individuals and 28 organisations. Below, you can view the full list of Nobel Prizes and Nobel Prize laureates.

2025
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2025/summary/)

[John Clarke](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2025/clarke/facts/), [Michel H. Devoret](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2025/devoret/facts/) and [John M. Martinis](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2025/martinis/facts/)

> “for the discovery of macroscopic quantum mechanical tunnelling and energy quantisation in an electric circuit”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2025/summary/)

[Susumu Kitagawa](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2025/kitagawa/facts/), [Richard Robson](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2025/robson/facts/) and [Omar M. Yaghi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2025/yaghi/facts/)

> “for the development of metal–organic frameworks”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2025/summary/)

[Mary E. Brunkow](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2025/brunkow/facts/), [Fred Ramsdell](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2025/ramsdell/facts/) and [Shimon Sakaguchi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2025/sakaguchi/facts/)

> “for their discoveries concerning peripheral immune tolerance”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2025/summary/)

[László Krasznahorkai](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2025/krasznahorkai/facts/)

> “for his compelling and visionary oeuvre that, in the midst of apocalyptic terror, reaffirms the power of art”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2025/summary/)

[Maria Corina Machado](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2025/machado/facts/)

> “for her tireless work promoting democratic rights for the people of Venezuela and for her struggle to achieve a just and peaceful transition from dictatorship to democracy”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2025](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2025/summary/)

> “for having explained innovation-driven economic growth”

[Joel Mokyr](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2025/mokyr/facts/)

> “for having identified the prerequisites for sustained growth through technological progress”

[Philippe Aghion](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2025/aghion/facts/) and [Peter Howitt](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2025/howitt/facts/)

> “for the theory of sustained growth through creative destruction”

2024
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2024/summary/)

[John J. Hopfield](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2024/hopfield/facts/) and [Geoffrey Hinton](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2024/hinton/facts/)

> “for foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2024/summary/)

[David Baker](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2024/baker/facts/)

> “for computational protein design”

[Demis Hassabis](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2024/hassabis/facts/) and [John Jumper](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2024/jumper/facts/)

> “for protein structure prediction”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2024/summary/)

[Victor Ambros](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2024/ambros/facts/) and [Gary Ruvkun](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2024/ruvkun/facts/)

> “for the discovery of microRNA and its role in post-transcriptional gene regulation”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2024/summary/)

[Han Kang](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2024/han/facts/)

> “for her intense poetic prose that confronts historical traumas and exposes the fragility of human life”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2024/summary/)

[Nihon Hidankyo](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2024/nihon-hidankyo/facts/)

> “for its efforts to achieve a world free of nuclear weapons and for demonstrating through witness testimony that nuclear weapons must never be used again”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2024](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2024/summary/)

[Daron Acemoglu](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2024/acemoglu/facts/), [Simon Johnson](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2024/johnson/facts/) and [James A. Robinson](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2024/robinson/facts/)

> “for studies of how institutions are formed and affect prosperity”

2023
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2023/summary/)

[Pierre Agostini](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2023/agostini/facts/), [Ferenc Krausz](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2023/krausz/facts/) and [Anne L’Huillier](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2023/lhuillier/facts/)

> “for experimental methods that generate attosecond pulses of light for the study of electron dynamics in matter”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2023/summary/)

[Moungi Bawendi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2023/bawendi/facts/), [Louis Brus](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2023/brus/facts/) and [Aleksey Yekimov](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2023/yekimov/facts/)

> “for the discovery and synthesis of quantum dots”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2023/summary/)

[Katalin Karikó](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2023/kariko/facts/) and [Drew Weissman](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2023/weissman/facts/)

> “for their discoveries concerning nucleoside base modifications that enabled the development of effective mRNA vaccines against COVID-19”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2023/summary/)

[Jon Fosse](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2023/fosse/facts/)

> “for his innovative plays and prose which give voice to the unsayable”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2023/summary/)

[Narges Mohammadi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2023/mohammadi/facts/)

> “for her fight against the oppression of women in Iran and her fight to promote human rights and freedom for all”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2023](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2023/summary/)

[Claudia Goldin](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2023/goldin/facts/)

> “for having advanced our understanding of women’s labour market outcomes”

2022
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2022/summary/)

[Alain Aspect](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2022/aspect/facts/), [John Clauser](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2022/clauser/facts/) and [Anton Zeilinger](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2022/zeilinger/facts/)

> “for experiments with entangled photons, establishing the violation of Bell inequalities and pioneering quantum information science”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2022/summary/)

[Carolyn Bertozzi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2022/bertozzi/facts/), [Morten Meldal](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2022/meldal/facts/) and [K. Barry Sharpless](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2022/sharpless/facts/)

> “for the development of click chemistry and bioorthogonal chemistry”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2022/summary/)

[Svante Pääbo](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2022/paabo/facts/)

> “for his discoveries concerning the genomes of extinct hominins and human evolution”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2022/summary/)

[Annie Ernaux](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2022/ernaux/facts/)

> “for the courage and clinical acuity with which she uncovers the roots, estrangements and collective restraints of personal memory”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2022/summary/)

[Ales Bialiatski](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2022/bialiatski/facts/), [Memorial](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2022/memorial/facts/) and [Center for Civil Liberties](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2022/center-for-civil-liberties/facts/)

> “The Peace Prize laureates represent civil society in their home countries. They have for many years promoted the right to criticise power and protect the fundamental rights of citizens. They have made an outstanding effort to document war crimes, human right abuses and the abuse of power. Together they demonstrate the significance of civil society for peace and democracy”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2022](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2022/summary/)

[Ben Bernanke](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2022/bernanke/facts/), [Douglas Diamond](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2022/diamond/facts/) and [Philip Dybvig](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2022/dybvig/facts/)

> “for research on banks and financial crises”

2021
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2021/summary/)

> “for groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of complex physical systems”

[Syukuro Manabe](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2021/manabe/facts/) and [Klaus Hasselmann](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2021/hasselmann/facts/)

> “for the physical modelling of Earth’s climate, quantifying variability and reliably predicting global warming”

[Giorgio Parisi](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2021/parisi/facts/)

> “for the discovery of the interplay of disorder and fluctuations in physical systems from atomic to planetary scales”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2021/summary/)

[Benjamin List](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2021/list/facts/) and [David W.C. MacMillan](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2021/macmillan/facts/)

> “for the development of asymmetric organocatalysis”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2021/summary/)

[David Julius](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2021/julius/facts/) and [Ardem Patapoutian](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2021/patapoutian/facts/)

> “for their discoveries of receptors for temperature and touch”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2021/summary/)

[Abdulrazak Gurnah](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2021/gurnah/facts/)

> “for his uncompromising and compassionate penetration of the effects of colonialism and the fate of the refugee in the gulf between cultures and continents”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2021/summary/)

[Maria Ressa](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2021/ressa/facts/) and [Dmitry Muratov](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2021/muratov/facts/)

> “for their efforts to safeguard freedom of expression, which is a precondition for democracy and lasting peace”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2021](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2021/summary/)

[David Card](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2021/card/facts/)

> “for his empirical contributions to labour economics”

[Joshua D. Angrist](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2021/angrist/facts/) and [Guido W. Imbens](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2021/imbens/facts/)

> “for their methodological contributions to the analysis of causal relationships”

2020
----

### [Nobel Prize in Physics 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2020/summary/)

[Roger Penrose](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2020/penrose/facts/)

> “for the discovery that black hole formation is a robust prediction of the general theory of relativity”

[Reinhard Genzel](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2020/genzel/facts/) and [Andrea Ghez](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2020/ghez/facts/)

> “for the discovery of a supermassive compact object at the centre of our galaxy”

### [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2020/summary/)

[Emmanuelle Charpentier](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2020/charpentier/facts/) and [Jennifer A. Doudna](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2020/doudna/facts/)

> “for the development of a method for genome editing”

### [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2020/summary/)

[Harvey J. Alter](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2020/alter/facts/), [Michael Houghton](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2020/houghton/facts/) and [Charles M. Rice](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2020/rice/facts/)

> “for the discovery of Hepatitis C virus”

### [Nobel Prize in Literature 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2020/summary/)

[Louise Glück](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/literature/2020/gluck/facts/)

> “for her unmistakable poetic voice that with austere beauty makes individual existence universal”

### [Nobel Peace Prize 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2020/summary/)

[World Food Programme](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/2020/wfp/facts/)

> “for its efforts to combat hunger, for its contribution to bettering conditions for peace in conflict-affected areas and for acting as a driving force in efforts to prevent the use of hunger as a weapon of war and conflict”

### [Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2020](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2020/summary/)

[Paul R. Milgrom](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2020/milgrom/facts/) and [Robert B. Wilson](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2020/wilson/facts/)

> “for improvements to auction theory and inventions of new auction formats”

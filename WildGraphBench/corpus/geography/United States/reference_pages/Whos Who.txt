Who's Who
---------

Administration

The American Theatre Wing's Tony Awards® are presented by Tony Award Productions, a joint venture of [The Broadway League](javascript:openExternal('http://www.broadwayleague.com',true);) and the [American Theatre Wing](javascript:openExternal('http://www.americantheatrewing.org',true);). The two organizations have jointly administered the Tonys since 1967, the year of the first Tony telecast.

Committees

[Management Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/mgt_comm.html)

[Administration Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/admin_comm.html)

[Nominating Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/nom_comm.html)

Responsibility for the governance and administration of the awards is vested in two committees:

The Tony Awards [Management Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/mgt_comm.html) is made up of representatives of the League and the Wing. It oversees the Tony Awards, with emphasis on the television broadcast.

The Tony Awards [Administration Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/admin_comm.html) is comprised of 24 members, of whom 10 are designated by the Wing, 10 by the League, and one each by the Dramatists Guild, Actors' Equity Association, United Scenic Artists, and the Society of Stage Directors and Choreographers. This committee determines eligibility for nominations in all awards categories and reviews the rules governing the awards. It may, at its discretion, bestow non-competitive Tony Awards including Special Tony Awards, the Regional Theatre Tony Award, and the Isabelle Stevenson Award; as well as Tony Honors for Excellence in the Theatre.

The Administration Comittee also appoints the Tony Awards [Nominating Committee](http://www.tonyawards.com/en_US/about/nom_comm.html), which is a rotating group of theatre professionals.

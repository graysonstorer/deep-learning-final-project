# Facts About Child Hunger in America | Feeding America

Facts about child hunger
------------------------

We all deserve to have enough food to eat without worrying about if we can afford it. At Feeding America, we are working to make this a reality.

*   ![Image 1: Orange List Icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2023-08/list-custom-orange.png?itok=PIE3pbf7)

### Nearly 14 million children faced hunger in 2023.

According to the [USDA](https://www.ers.usda.gov/topics/food-nutrition-assistance/food-security-in-the-u-s/), one in every five children is unsure where they will get their next meal. 
*   ![Image 2: Orange List Icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2023-08/list-custom-orange.png?itok=PIE3pbf7)

### Families with children are more likely to face hunger.

Raising kids can cost a lot of money. Buying clothes, getting food, and paying for school can all add up fast and make it tough for families to meet all their needs. 
*   ![Image 3: Orange List Icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2023-08/list-custom-orange.png?itok=PIE3pbf7)

### Black and Latino children are nearly twice as likely to face hunger.

Many families, regardless of their race, have a hard time affording food. However, it happens more frequently to families of color because of discrimination based on race. 
*   ![Image 4: Orange List Icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2023-08/list-custom-orange.png?itok=PIE3pbf7)

### Single-parent families are more likely to face hunger.

Managing expenses can be tough with one source of income. In 2023, 1 in 3 households headed by single moms and 1 in 4 households headed by single dads experienced food insecurity. 

Causes of child hunger
----------------------

There are many reasons why kids might not have enough to eat. Some of the common reasons include:

![Image 5: Orange briefcase](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2019-07/FA_Icons_Employment_Org.png?itok=4UHGIoM3)

### Low family incomes

Although parents and caregivers work hard to provide for their children, low wages make it difficult to have enough money to buy food.

![Image 6: Home Icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/assets/images/icons/food-waste-icon-homes.png?itok=N2XCvnYH)

### Lack of affordable housing

Despite budgeting and saving, the high cost of housing can make it difficult for families with children to afford food and rent.

![Image 7: Three people](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/2019-05/Food%20Insecurity%20Rate%20Icon.jpg?itok=B630YwRf)

### Racism and discrimination

Black people, Latinos, and Native Americans are more likely to face barriers to getting food.

Effects of child hunger
-----------------------

Hunger can affect children's health, development, and well-being. Some common effects include:

![Image 8: Green silhouette of a person's head and shoulders inside a white circle, with a question mark floating above the head](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/assets/images/icon-fundrasing-step1.png?itok=-qnA0Q2D)

### Poor academic performance

Children who experience food insecurity may have trouble focusing and learning in school.

![Image 9: fruit icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/assets/images/icons/fruits-icon.png?itok=m4MBJHO8)

### Increased health problems

Kids who don't get enough food to eat face higher risks of health conditions like anemia and asthma.

![Image 10: Olive drab and black tool icon](https://www.feedingamerica.org/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/assets/images/icons/Plate-icon-green.png?itok=Ng_xLRJ4)

### Developmental differences

Hunger can change how a child's brain and body grow and affect their thinking, learning, and behavior.

Ending child hunger
-------------------

When children have enough food, they can concentrate on important things like doing well in school, playing sports, and graduating.

We support programs that provide food to children after school, over the weekend and during the summer.

Spanish-language daily newspapers
---------------------------------

*   ### [La Opinión](https://laopinion.com/) (Los Angeles, California )

Largest Spanish-language daily newspaper published in the United States. The newspaper was founded in 1926.

*   ### [Los Angeles Times: Español](https://www.latimes.com/espanol/)

*   ### [El Diario La Prensa](https://eldiariony.com/) (New York)

One of the oldest Spanish-language daily newspapers in the United States. The newspaper owned by impreMedia LLC.

*   ### [El Nuevo Herald](https://www.elnuevoherald.com/) (Miami, Florida)

*   ### [Las Américas Newspaper](https://lasamericasnews.com/)

US Hispanic newspaper based in Falls Church, Virginia. The newspaper established in 1999, circulating in VA, MD, and DC metropolitan area.

Spanish-language newspapers in the United States play a vital role in serving the nation's large and diverse Spanish-speaking population. Prominent publications such as _La Opinión_, _El Nuevo Herald_, _El Diario NY_, _Hoy_, _La Voz de Houston_, and _Al Día Dallas_ provide a broad spectrum of content—from breaking news and political commentary to lifestyle, health, and entertainment. These outlets are instrumental in keeping Hispanic communities informed, engaged, and represented in the American media landscape.

Focused on regions with high Hispanic populations, these newspapers are widely read in cities such as Los Angeles, Miami, New York, and Dallas. They deliver relevant local, national, and international coverage while celebrating Hispanic heritage and addressing community-specific concerns. By offering news in Spanish, they ensure critical information remains accessible, empowering readers to stay informed and connected—both culturally and civically—within the broader U.S. society.

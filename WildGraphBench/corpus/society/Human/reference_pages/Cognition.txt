cognition
---------

Pronunciation: /kɒɡˈnɪʃ(ə)n/ [](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/words/key-to-pronunciation)

noun
----

_[mass noun]_

[](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)1 The mental action or process of [acquiring](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/acquire#acquire__2 "Meaning of acquiring")[knowledge](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/knowledge#knowledge__8 "Meaning of knowledge") and [understanding](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/understanding#understanding__4 "Meaning of understanding") through thought, experience, and the [senses](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/sense#sense__5 "Meaning of senses").

[Example sentences](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)
*   By metacognition I mean knowledge about cognition itself and control of one's own cognitive processes.
*   The findings from these experiments have been taken to demonstrate the role of cognition in the experience of emotion.
*   In itself the a priori has nothing whatever to do with thinking and cognition.
*   [Get more examples](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/products)

[](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)1.1 _[count noun]_ A [perception](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/perception#perception__2 "Meaning of perception"), [sensation](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/sensation#sensation__2 "Meaning of sensation"), idea, or [intuition](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/intuition#intuition__2 "Meaning of intuition")[resulting](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/result#result__10 "Meaning of resulting") from the process of cognition.

[Example sentences](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)
*   That makes it at least plausible for a social cognitive premise that views prejudicial or stereotype-laden cognitions as largely unavoidable for most humans.
*   Dissonance occurs when ever a person holds inconsistent cognitions (eg opinions, beliefs or behaviours).
*   The premise is that individuals strive toward consistency between cognitions by changing their opinions or beliefs to make them more consistent with each other.
*   [Get more examples](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/products)

Derivatives
-----------

[](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)

#### cognitional

adjective

[](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)

[Example sentences](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/cognition)
*   Any philosophy will rest upon the operative methods of cognitional activity, either as correctly conceived or as distorted by oversights and mistaken orientations.
*   A person's answer to these questions will be their cognitional theory, their epistemology, and their metaphysics.
*   This is a logical or cognitional distinction, which does not necessarily reflect anything in the nature of things.
*   [Get more examples](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/products)

Origin
------

Late Middle English: from [Latin](http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/Latin#Latin__2 "Meaning of Latin")_cognitio(-_), from _cognoscere_ 'get to know'.

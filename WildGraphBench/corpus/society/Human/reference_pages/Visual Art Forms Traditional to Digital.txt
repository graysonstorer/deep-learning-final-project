Visual Art Forms: Traditional to Digital ======================================== [![Image 7: Front Cover](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content?id=tBqgBQAAQBAJ&printsec=frontcover&img=1&zoom=1&edge=curl&imgtk=AFLRE73gIoZ18bzLJ9Q_JECAV7QXGCzeBNGRMKdhxRoI5BNdLPMI9ws1dCX77-MwVeWa2w9yuESoPAoGP5ljqvpFU2mEkqTr15QXJm_2DLECwGApX034TbvqEQCyqZzmTrzfXaaJyp-E)](https://books.google.com/books?id=tBqgBQAAQBAJ&printsec=frontcover&source=gbs_ge_summary_r&cad=0) [Sergey Mavrody](https://www.google.com/search?tbo=p&tbm=bks&q=inauthor:%22Sergey+Mavrody%22) Sergey's HTML5 & CSS3 The visual arts are art forms that create works that are primarily visual in nature, such as ceramics, drawing, painting, sculpture, printmaking, design, crafts, photography, video, animation and architecture. These definitions should not be taken too strictly as many artistic disciplines (performing arts, conceptual art, textile arts) involve aspects of the visual arts as well as the arts of other types. Also included within the visual arts are the applied arts such as industrial design, graphic design, fashion design, interior design and decorative art. This unique monograph has over 500 images, illustrating various visualart forms using examples from a single author. The book topics include: Painting, Drawing and Sketching, Sculpture, Illustration, Film, Visual Communications, Infographics, CGI and 3-D Imaging, Interaction Design, User Interface Design, Information Architecture. ### [](https://books.google.com/books?id=tBqgBQAAQBAJ)About the author

Sergey A. Mavrody has been working with web technologies since the mid-nineties, focusing on UI design and development, creative direction, information architecture, interactive media, and enterprise applications with rich data visualization and advanced UI components.

Sergey holds two Master's degrees. In 1990 Sergey received a Master of Fine Arts in Art Directing and Animation from the Gerasimov National University of Motion Pictures (VGIK). In 1992 he graduated from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, earning his second Master's.

Sergey is also a visual artist, chess master and educator with many years experience teaching as a professor at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, and at the DeVry University.

**Judging from the shape, the types of literary arts consist of 4 forms, namely:**

**1. Prose**

Prose is a form of literary art that is described by using language that is free and tends not to be bound by rhythm, diction, rhyme, soundness or rules and other literary guidelines. This type of prose writing is usually used to describe a fact or idea. Because prose can be used for newspapers, magazines, novels, encyclopedias, letters, and various other types of media. Prose is divided into four types, namely narrative prose, descriptive prose, exposition prose, and argumentative prose.

The form of prose itself has two kinds, namely romance and novel. Romance is a story that tells a character as a whole from birth to the end of his life, while the novel only tells a part of the life of a character who changes his destiny.

**2. Poetry**

Poetry is a literary work that is described using diction or words of choice, characterized by a dense but beautiful discussion, usually poetry works can indirectly cause a tendency of someone to sharpen their awareness through language that has a rhythm and special meaning. Examples of poetry such as poetry, rhymes, ballads.

**Intrinsic elements of poetry include:**

– Diction is the words chosen by a poet in creating poetry. These words are certainly words that express beauty and feelings.

 – The image is the effort of the poet in arousing the imagination / imagination of the reader about the event or feeling experienced by the poet so that the reader can feel it.

 – Majas, namely the expression of the language chosen by the poet to clarify the intent. Express with a picture / figuratively, make freshness, and cause clarity of feeling.

 – Rima is the equation of sound in poetry that is useful to clarify the intentions and lead to decisions.

 – Rhythm is alternating up and down, long-short pronunciation of poetic language on a regular basis.

**Extrinsic elements of poetry include:**

Example: Author’s education, author’s history, author’s religion, and author’s background.

**Poetry reading techniques include:**

– Normal speech and movements. don’t have to be made up.

 – Pronunciation must be clear.

**Terms for reading good poetry include:**

– Understand the contents of poetry.

 – Proper articulation and intonation.

 – Give pause pressure on important words.

 – Spell words clearly with expressions that match what is conveyed.

**3. Drama**

Drama is a form of literature that is described using language that is free and long, and is presented using dialogue or monologue. drama has two meanings, namely drama in the form of script or drama that is staged.

**Various types of drama:**

1. Comedy is a story that contains humor, jokes that can entertain the audience.

 2. Tragedy is a story in which contains difficulties or difficulties experienced by the characters.

 3. Comedy tragedy is a story that contains difficulties and humor / humor in turns.

 4. Opera / musical is a drama that is accompanied by music as a complement to the performance of the art.

**Judging from the contents of literary arts consists of 4 types, namely:**

1. Epic, essay that describes something objectively without following the author’s personal thoughts and feelings

2. Lyrics, essays containing subjective feelings of the author.

3. Dididak, literary works whose contents are moral messages, etiquette, religion, and so on.

4. Dramatic, a literary work whose contents depict an event with an exaggerated picture.

**Judging from its history, literary works consist of two parts, namely:**

**1. Old Literature**

Old literature is literary works produced by writers who were in the era of the kingdom or where there was no national movement. Old literature consists of poetry, fairy tales, and saga.

a. rhymes, the type of literary work in the form of fragments of biased sentences consisting of four sentences of advice, custom, or religious teachings in which have the form of the sentence ending in harmony. Pantun is an old poem form with characteristics like:

– Each verse consists of 4 lines

 – Each row consists of 8 to. 12 syllables

 – rhyming a b a b

 – Lines 1 and 2 are sampiran

 – Lines 3 and 4 are contents

**Example:**

When the light has changed

 The evening must have come

 If you have no heart

 There is no way I would propose

In the example above we see that it has rhymed a b a b, and rows 1 and 2 are sampiran, while rows 3 and 4 are contents.

b. Fairy tales, old literary works in the form of fiction. This tale has several types, among which are legends. furniture, myth, sage, and humorous stories.

c. Saga, saga is old literature in the form of prose which is usually sourced from the stories of kings or gods.

**2. Modern Literature**

Modern literature is literary works that live and develop in the life of modern society. Sastramodern was born after the emergence of the national movement. Modern literature itself is usually in the form of poetry, prose, short stories, novels, romance, and drama.

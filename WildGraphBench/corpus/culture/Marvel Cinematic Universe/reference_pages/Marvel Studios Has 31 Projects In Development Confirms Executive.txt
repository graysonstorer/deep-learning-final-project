Marvel Studios Has 31 Projects In Development Confirms Executive
================================================================

In a new interview, Marvel executive Victoria Alonso confirms that Marvel Studios has around 31 projects currently in development for the future.

[By Nicholas Graff](https://screenrant.com/author/nicholas-graff/)Published 15 minutes ago

![Image 5: Spider Man No Way Home Tom Holland 4K IMAX trailer](https://screenrant.com/marvel-studios-mcu-upcoming-projects-development/)

A Marvel executive has confirmed that the studio has 31 more projects in development for the future of the **[MCU](https://screenrant.com/tag/mcu/)**. With all of the post-pandemic releases this year many may have thought Marvel was just catching up with films they could not release in 2020. However, it looks like the studio is just getting started in ramping up their ever-expanding lineup of movies and Disney+ series.

When the Marvel Cinematic Universe [MCU] first launched back in 2008 with the first _Iron Man_, fans became accustomed to a status quo of about 1 movie a year, occasionally two. After _The Avengers_, standard procedure was for Marvel Studios to release 2 films a year, which already felt like a lot of content for audiences. And it only continued from there, as _Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2_ began the first year of three MCU films in 2017.This yearâ€™s _Black Widow_[began the first year of four films](https://screenrant.com/marvel-4-mcu-films-year/), which will continue in 2022. But with all their success, and the fans wanting more, it only made sense to increase production.

How many of the 31 projects has Marvel made public already? Counting the nine remaining films of Phase 4 (ending in _Fantastic Four)_, the two Phase 5 films (_Captain America 4_ and _Blade)_, and the nine [TV series that have yet to debut](https://screenrant.com/marvel-shows-future-season-2-disney-plus-feige/), puts the number around 20. That means there are at least 11 more projects that Marvel has kept tightly under wraps.

The big fear is that somewhere along the line the quality will start to dip, but hopefully with Kevin Feige at the helm, he will continue to bring in fresh talent and steer the ship in the right direction. But even Feige can only hold the course for so long. Until then, audiences get to sit back and enjoy the ride as wave after wave of Marvel content hits theaters and Disney+ in the coming years.

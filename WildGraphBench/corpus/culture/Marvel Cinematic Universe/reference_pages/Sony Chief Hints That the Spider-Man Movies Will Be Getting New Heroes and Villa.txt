Sony Chief Hints That the Spider-Man Movies Will Be Getting New Heroes and Villains
===================================================================================

Amy Pascal says studio will access Marvel’s full world of Spidey characters.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

by Scott Collura

 November 21, 2013

[Submit](http://www.reddit.com/submit)[Tweet](https://twitter.com/share)[Share](https://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php)[+1](https://plus.google.com/share)[Share](http://www.tumblr.com/share)

[Submit](http://www.reddit.com/submit)[Tweet](https://twitter.com/share)[Share](https://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php)[+1](https://plus.google.com/share)[Share](http://www.tumblr.com/share)

Standalone superhero movies are old news. It’s all about universe building these days, yo!

[_**What Other Heroes Could Join the Amazing Spider-Man Films?**_](http://www.ign.com/articles/2013/08/02/what-other-heroes-could-join-the-amazing-spider-man-films)

Yes, the rest of Hollywood has seen the success of Marvel’s amazing output (and business model), and they’re trying to replicate it. Take Fox’s X-Men: Days of Future Past, which appears to be a first step towards creating a more complex, interactive array of mutant characters on the big screen. And then there’s Sony, which certainly seems to have a similar idea regarding their Spider-Man films.

During an investor presentation today, Sony Pictures Entertainment Co-Chairman Amy Pascal hinted that there are big plans in place for Spidey’s universe.

“We are going to access Marvel’s full world of Spider-Man characters, so be on the lookout for new heroes and villains,” Pascal said.

Whether that means spin-off films or just loads of Spider-Man sequels with various characters is unclear. Of course, this has been in the air for a while now, with hints of the current films featuring [Andrew Garfield](http://www.ign.com/stars/andrew-garfield "Andrew Garfield") possibly leading up to a Sinister Six appearance. And director [Marc Webb](http://www.ign.com/stars/marc-webb "Marc Webb") has [implied](http://www.ign.com/articles/2013/07/25/the-amazing-spider-man-4-may-be-a-spin-off-movie) that Amazing Spider-Man 4 could be more of a spin-off film.

“I think [for] the fourth movie, what we've discovered is there are so many ancillary characters, that have enormous, cinematic potential that there may be other ways to exploit those characters, in a way that is exciting and fun and worthwhile. It might not just be a Spider-Man movie,” he said back in July.

In the meantime, [The Amazing Spider-Man 2](http://www.ign.com/movies/the-amazing-spider-man-2 "The Amazing Spider-Man 2") will be released on May 2, 2014. Wanna bet there will be an after-credits Easter Egg?

Marvel Avengers Update
======================

_by_**[Mark Beall](https://www.cinematical.com/bloggers/mark-beall/)** Sep 14th 2006 // 11:32AM

Filed under: [Action](https://www.cinematical.com/category/action-and-adventure/), [Deals](https://www.cinematical.com/category/deals/), [Fandom](https://www.cinematical.com/category/fandom/), [Scripts](https://www.cinematical.com/category/scripts-and-screenwriting/), [Distribution](https://www.cinematical.com/category/distribution/), [Comic/Superhero/Geek](https://www.cinematical.com/category/comic-superhero-geek/), [Remakes and Sequels](https://www.cinematical.com/category/remakes-and-sequels/)

![Image 4](http://www.blogcdn.com/www.cinematical.com/media/2006/09/cappy.jpg)Marvel Comics has been making grand plans for their $700 million war chest, and news of new Marvel properties headed to the silver screen continues to grow. When rumors started becoming fact about movies such as [_Iron Man_](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/06/21/favreau-on-iron-man/), [_Captain America_](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/02/17/captain-america-finds-a-scribe/), [_Ant-Man_](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/04/29/marvel-on-the-move-edgar-wright-is-official-for-ant-man/), [_Thor_](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/07/06/david-goyer-gets-thor/), a revamp of [_The Incredible Hulk_](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/06/13/mark-bealls-geek-beat-the-incredible-hulk-redux/), and possibly even a _[Black Panther](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/07/12/new-name-floated-for-marvels-black-panther/)_ movie, everyone familiar with the comics world said "[hey, somebody is gearing up for a big ol' _Avengers_ movie](http://www.cinematical.com/2006/05/02/mark-bealls-geek-beat-crossover-action-desired/)." And surprise, surprise, when Marvel announced their future slate, _The Avengers_ was included in the plans.

In a brief presentation to Wall Street analysts, Marvel confirmed their crossover plans. Essentially, what Marvel said boiled down to releasing several individual movies first to establish firm characters (and presumably get intro stories out of the way for everyone) and create familiarity with the non-Marvel crowd, and then following up with a big _Avengers_ movie. All of the above mentioned heroes were Avengers at one point -- many of them originals. This sounds like a great way for Marvel to utilize one of their final tent-pole properties. What do you think?

The Avengers lack the outsider allure of the X-Men and don't have the same level of familial closeness, but I think the audience would be there. It has taken years to get audiences used to taking comic characters seriously, so if it's to be done 'twere well done quickly.

Having developed some knowledge and sophistication with the superhero universe, let's hope this paves the way for the more mature graphic novels like Watchmen, the Invisibles or Top Ten. I'd love to see a film version of Gaiman's run on 1602.

# Going viral: Timeline shows how COVID-19 infected Queensland

**January 25:** Australia’s [first confirmed case,](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/operating-in-the-dark-a-year-since-australia-s-first-public-covid-19-concerns-20210118-p56uyp.html) a man who had flown to Melbourne.

**January 28:** A Chinese man [tests positive](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/chinese-football-team-quarantined-in-brisbane-hotel-20200129-p53vxu.html) for the virus on the Gold Coast, and is Queensland’s first confirmed case. At this stage there are four confirmed coronavirus cases in NSW and two in Victoria.

**January 29:** Queensland declares a public health emergency as the Chinese women's soccer team is put into[hotel quarantine](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/sport/soccer/olympic-qualifiers-in-doubt-with-chinese-team-kept-in-hotel-quarantine-20200129-p53vu2.html) in Brisbane.

**January 30:** A 42-year-old Chinese woman becomes Queensland’s second case.

**January 31:** Queensland Premier Annastacia Palaszczuk[accuses](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-frustrated-over-federal-government-coronavirus-response-20200131-p53wjy.html) the federal government of withholding vital information about the coronavirus outbreak.

### February

**February 1:** Queensland Health begins to[isolate](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/eight-students-at-seq-private-school-isolated-over-coronavirus-concerns-20200201-p53wu2.html) school students and boarders arriving back from China.

**February 4:**An eight-year-old boy becomes the first child to test positive in Queensland. He is part of the Wuhan tour group visiting the Gold Coast. He is [released from isolation](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/boy-released-from-coronavirus-quarantine-at-gold-coast-hospital-20200220-p542m7.html) 16 days later, making him the first person in the state to recover from the virus.

**February 6:**[Emergency laws](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/emergency-health-powers-extended-as-quarantined-team-leaves-brisbane-20200206-p53ybd.html) rushed through Parliament give Chief Health Officer Jeannette Young powers to put people into quarantine against their will and force others to be tested.

An official sanitises voting areas during the local government election in Brisbane in March.Jono Searle/Getty Images

**February 11:**The World Health Organisation announces the coronavirus strain will be named SARS-CoV-2 and the disease it causes will be COVID-19.

**February 26:** Former treasurer Jackie Trad says the pandemic [might cost](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/cornavirus-to-take-a-billion-dollar-bite-out-of-queensland-s-bottom-line-20200226-p544kz.html) the Queensland economy up to $1 billion.

### March

**March 1:** Western Australian tourism pioneer James Kwan is the [first Australian to die](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/man-becomes-first-australian-to-die-as-coronavirus-spreads-20200301-p545tm.html) from the new coronavirus.

**March 3:** Queensland tally hits 10 after an international student studying at the University of Queensland [tests positive.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/university-student-tests-positive-for-coronavirus-in-brisbane-20200303-p546ay.html)

**March 11:** WHO [declares](https://www.smh.com.au/world/asia/world-health-organisation-declares-covid-19-outbreak-a-pandemic-20200312-p5497k.html) a global pandemic.

**March 12:** Double Oscar winner Tom Hanks and his actor-singer wife Rita Wilson [test positive](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/culture/movies/tom-hanks-and-wife-rita-wilson-diagnosed-with-coronavirus-in-australia-20200312-p549dj.html) for COVID-19 on the Gold Coast.

**March 13:** Home Affairs Minister Peter Dutton is admitted to a Brisbane hospital after [contracting the disease.](https://www.smh.com.au/politics/federal/home-affairs-minister-peter-dutton-has-coronavirus-20200313-p549y9.html)

**March 15:** Health authorities confirm Queensland's [first COVID-19 death](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/sunshine-coast-woman-77-becomes-australia-s-fourth-coronavirus-death-20200315-p54ab6.html), a 77-year-old woman from the Sunshine Coast who died after falling ill on a flight from Brisbane to Sydney.

**March 16:** Queensland senator Susan McDonald [tests positive.](https://www.smh.com.au/politics/federal/covid-19-was-like-a-dry-throat-in-a-dusty-cattle-yard-senator-says-20200318-p54bdr.html) UQ and Griffith University [suspend lectures.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/uq-suspends-teaching-for-one-week-amid-escalating-coronavirus-concerns-20200316-p54ada.html) All people entering Australia must self-isolate for 14 days.

**March 17:** Police hit pause on all static random breath tests and jury trials are suspended.

**March 18:** More emergency [laws pass](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/this-is-a-deadly-virus-emergency-laws-to-slow-outbreak-in-queensland-20200318-p54bj8.html) to allow Queensland delivery trucks to restock supermarkets 24/7 to curb effects of panic-buying, and Indigenous communities begin to [ban travel.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/remote-queensland-communities-begin-restricting-travel-20200318-p54be3.html)

> “The very way we interact with each other must change, and it must change today.”
> 
> Premier Annastacia Palaszczuk, March 18, 2020

**March 19:**The infamous Ruby Princess cruise ship docks in Sydney Harbour. The NSW government[assesses](https://www.smh.com.au/national/ruby-princess-health-log-updated-after-passengers-disembarked-20200422-p54m7h.html) the ship as “low risk” despite more than 100 passengers [presenting with](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/nsw/decision-to-allow-ruby-princess-passengers-ashore-based-on-outdated-log-20200505-p54pyg.html) acute respiratory symptoms. The ship [spawns 900 COVID-cases](https://www.smh.com.au/national/nsw/return-to-troubled-waters-inquiry-to-report-on-ruby-princess-fallout-20200812-p55kxl.html)and 22 people die.

**March 20:** Australia’s [borders close](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/lifestyle/health-and-wellness/coronavirus-australia-closes-its-borders-20200320-p54ceg.html) to all non-residents as Queensland’s COVID tally hits 184. Queensland coronial inquests are adjourned, [elective surgeries](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/elective-surgery-held-off-to-make-room-for-flood-of-virus-patients-20200320-p54c6k.html) are postponed and school NAPLAN [tests are scrapped](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/education-minister-grace-grace-pushes-to-cancel-naplan-2020-20200320-p54c3f.html). The national cabinet agrees to limits of [one person per four square metres](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/federal-budget-delayed-new-restrictions-on-indoor-gatherings-australia-20200320-p54c9m.html) in indoor venues and Palaszczuk tells people [not to go to pubs.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/like-being-hit-by-30-cyclones-at-once-premier-says-don-t-go-to-the-pub-20200320-p54cdc.html)[](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/education-minister-grace-grace-pushes-to-cancel-naplan-2020-20200320-p54c3f.html)

**March 22:** Palaszczuk pledges [$17 million in funding](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/premier-injects-17-million-to-fast-track-coronavirus-vaccine-20200322-p54cn9.html) to help UQ researchers fast-track a vaccine while Prime Minister Scott Morrison announces the JobKeeper wage subsidy scheme.

Long queues plague motorists arriving at Queensland border checkpoints.Dan Peled

**March 23:** Australia enters the first stage of a [nation-wide shutdown](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/lifestyle/health-and-wellness/coronavirus-australia-commences-stage-one-of-shutdown-20200323-p54cth.html), which closes restaurants, pubs, clubs and gyms. Thousands of workers lose their jobs.

**March 24:**Guests are banned from weddings and no more than 10 mourners can [attend funerals.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/how-the-coronavirus-pandemic-is-changing-funerals-20200323-p54d01.html) State government unveils $4 billion [rescue package](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/tax-holiday-first-stage-of-queensland-s-4-billion-rescue-package-20200323-p54d5d.html).

**March 25:**Toowoomba man [Garry Kirstenfeldt](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/family-never-though-garry-s-17th-cruise-would-be-his-last-20200327-p54eke.html), 68, becomes the ninth Australian to die after contracting SARS-CoV-2 on a cruise ship. He dies alone in intensive care and is the virus' second Queensland victim.

Garry Kirstenfeldt was the ninth Australian to die from coronavirus.

**March 26:**Queensland [closes](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/motorists-face-hours-of-delays-as-queensland-s-borders-are-locked-down-20200325-p54dyo.html) its borders for the first time in 100 years. Thousands of Australians are [stranded on cruise ships](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/brisbane-couple-trapped-on-cruise-as-business-falls-to-pieces-20200327-p54eo7.html) overseas.

**March 28:** Hotel quarantine is introduced, with taxpayers footing the bill. Queensland council elections and two state byelections are held as 70 new cases are confirmed in a single day. There are 270,000 people [who fail to cast a ballot.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/no-fine-for-those-too-scared-of-covid-to-vote-in-queensland-council-election-20200805-p55ivy.html)

**March 29:**[Karla Lake](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/third-covid-19-death-in-queensland-as-rna-prepped-to-take-patients-20200329-p54f0n.html) dies from COVID-19 in Caboolture Hospital after travelling on the Ruby Princess. Palaszczuk says Brisbane’s RNA showgrounds will become a [makeshift hospital](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/third-covid-19-death-in-queensland-as-rna-prepped-to-take-patients-20200329-p54f0n.html) if the cases surge out of control.

**March 30:** Federal government's [wage subsidy scheme](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/cushioning-the-decline-rba-finds-jobkeeper-saved-700-000-jobs-20201123-p56h0j.html) JobKeeper starts as Queensland's tally reaches 689 cases. Only people living alone are allowed visitors. Couples living in separate sharehouses are told they [can not see one another.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/no-visitors-allowed-with-1300-fine-for-those-who-leave-home-to-socialise-20200330-p54fcs.html)

### April

**April 2:**[Des Williams](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/three-of-four-covid-19-victims-in-queensland-returned-from-cruise-ships-20200402-p54gcr.html) becomes the fourth Queenslander to die while infected with the virus after disembarking the Ruby Princess. Federal government offers [free childcare.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/free-childcare-for-all-but-morrison-vows-to-go-back-to-old-ways-20200402-p54gfc.html)

**April 3:** State authorities change [visiting rules](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/rule-backflip-queensland-households-now-allowed-two-visitors-20200402-p54gh2.html) after widespread confusion. Another 39 people test positive lifting the total cases to 873. Domestic violence advocates [warn of crisis](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/dangerous-time-for-women-ticking-time-bomb-locked-inside-queensland-homes-20200401-p54g49.html) as victims are locked inside with abusers.

**April 5:** Ruby Princess outbreak claims another life. A [78-year-old man](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/qld-company-to-churn-out-face-masks-as-state-sees-smallest-case-jump-in-weeks-20200405-p54h7p.html) dies in Prince Charles Hospital and is Queensland’s fifth COVID victim.

**April 6:** Eight workers at the Ipswich Hospital [pathology laboratory](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/hospital-staff-quarantined-as-testing-is-expanded-in-parts-of-queensland-20200406-p54hmq.html) go into quarantine after coming into contact with a confirmed case.

**April 7:** A nurse who treated COVID patients at Brisbane’s Princess Alexandra Hospital is one of 13 people to [test positive.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-nurse-tests-positive-for-coronavirus-six-others-isolated-20200407-p54i00.html)

**April 8:**Modelling reveals 30,000 Queenslanders [would have died](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/modelling-shows-30-000-queenslanders-would-have-died-without-restrictions-20200408-p54i89.html) from COVID-19 if strict distancing laws were not introduced. Doctors are banned from prescribing [hydroxychloroquine](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/doctors-banned-from-prescribing-potential-covid-19-drug-20200408-p54ic1.html), spruiked by then-US president Donald Trump as a cure.

> “I think we’re going to smash the curve.”
> 
> Annastacia Palaszczuk, April 13, 2020

**April 13:** Palaszczuk announces state and private schools will be mostly [pupil-free](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/teachers-head-back-to-classrooms-for-term-2-but-schools-stay-online-20200413-p54jc9.html) for the first half of term two.

**April 14:** In a rare act of bipartisanship, Palaszczuk, a Labor premier, [lauds](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/he-is-doing-an-outstanding-job-queensland-premier-heaps-praise-on-pm-20200414-p54jle.html) Liberal Prime Minister Scott Morrison’s leadership during the coronavirus crisis.

**April 16:**Queensland’s number of [confirmed coronavirus](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/not-over-the-worst-of-it-why-queensland-is-braced-for-a-case-spike-20200416-p54kcu.html) cases pushes past 1000.

**April 18:** A sixth Queenslander dies from COVID-19 while in quarantine in Sydney. The[83-year-old man](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-records-sixth-covid-19-death-an-83-year-old-cruise-ship-passenger-20200418-p54l0a.html) had been a passenger on the Celebrity Eclipse cruise ship.

**April 20**: Queensland and NSW enter a [bidding war](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/we-will-bring-a-bazooka-states-in-a-bidding-war-for-virgin-hq-20200420-p54lbr.html) for Virgin Australia headquarters after the airline goes into voluntary administration, which Queensland [wins](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/when-virgin-flies-again-it-will-fly-from-queensland-treasurer-20200626-p556kc.html). The Queensland school [websites crashes](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-s-education-websites-crash-despite-weeks-to-prepare-20200420-p54ldw.html) as 1.8 million students try to learn from home and the state records the first day of [zero cases](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/zero-new-queensland-virus-cases-tremendous-but-restrictions-to-stay-20200420-p54ljb.html) since lockdown.

**April 21:**Five hospital lab workers [test positive](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/fears-for-cairns-cluster-after-5-hospital-lab-workers-test-positive-20200421-p54lpc.html) in Cairns.

**April 22:** A scaled-back Queensland Parliament sits for a snap 13-hour sitting to debate and pass more [emergency laws.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/many-people-are-fearful-mps-push-through-laws-as-elderly-seek-wills-20200422-p54m6o.html)

Queensland Premier Annastacia Palaszczuk sanitises her hands at Parliament House. Jono Searle/Getty Images

**April 24**: Gold Coast MP-turned Opposition Leader David Crisafulli slams Dr Young after she decides to [cancel an Anzac Day fly-over.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/power-hungry-lnp-slams-queensland-top-doctor-s-coronavirus-advice-20200424-p54mxo.html) He tells broadcaster Alan Jones: “This is punch-drunk bureaucrats who are power hungry and it needs to be called out.”

**April 26:**Palaszczuk announces the [first round](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-lockdown-restrictions-eased-here-s-what-you-re-allowed-to-do-20200426-p54naw.html) of easing restrictions. People will be allowed to leave home to go for a drive, picnic and shop for non-essential goods, such as clothing. Federal government releases the [COVIDSafe app.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/australians-urged-to-stick-with-covidsafe-app-20210108-p56spa.html)

**April 28:**Toowoomba North MP Trevor Watts resigns as Queensland’s opposition police spokesman after [being fined](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/mp-told-others-to-stay-home-days-before-he-was-fined-at-street-party-20200429-p54o64.html) for breaching coronavirus restrictions by attending a street party.

**April 30:** Queensland’s [unemployment rate](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/nobody-alive-has-ever-seen-this-129-500-queenslanders-lose-jobs-20200514-p54t0t.html) jumps to 6.8 per cent after the state’s economy lost 129,500 workers.

### May

**May 4:** Palaszczuk announces Queensland children will[return to the classroom](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-children-to-return-to-classroom-in-two-stages-starting-next-week-20200504-p54pjp.html) in two stages, starting May 11.

**May 7:**Dr Young announces two households will be allowed to [congregate together](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-restrictions-eased-for-mothers-day-here-s-what-is-allowed-20200507-p54qnz.html), just in time for Mother’s Day.

**May 11:** Queensland’s then-health minister [Steven Miles](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/queensland-has-a-new-deputy-premier-and-treasurer-who-are-they-20200511-p54rrp.html) is sworn in as deputy premier.

> “This is the worst global economic crisis we have seen. Nobody alive on the planet has ever seen this.”
> 
> Treasurer Cameron Dick, May 14, 2020

**May 15:** Restaurants, pubs, clubs, RSLs and cafes can [reopen](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/when-queenslanders-can-go-to-the-pub-salon-pool-and-gym-20200508-p54r5m.html) to 10 patrons at a time. A Rockhampton nurse working at a state-run aged care facility [tests positive](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-covid-positive-aged-care-nurse-worked-while-unwell-delayed-test-20200515-p54tax.html), sending the centre into lockdown.

**May 18:** Palaszczuk announces borders [could stay closed until September](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/federal/border-friction-new-zealand-set-to-open-for-nsw-victorian-tourists-before-queensland-20200524-p54vwg.html), kicking off the long-running border war between herself and the NSW Premier _._

**May 26:**One Nation leader Pauline Hanson [imposes a “deadline”](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/pauline-hanson-imposes-deadline-of-thursday-to-reopen-queensland-borders-20200526-p54wnz.html) on the Queensland government to lift border restrictions or face a constitutional challenge in the High Court.

**May 27:** Nathan Turner, 30, [dies at his home](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-s-seventh-covid-19-victim-identified-20200527-p54x1v.html) in Blackwater. He tests positive to COVID-19 following an autopsy. Thousands of people in central Queensland come forward for testing.

**May 31:**Queenslanders are [allowed to travel](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-lifts-ban-on-travel-within-state-but-borders-remain-shut-20200531-p54y42.html) around the state. During May, the state’s unemployment [jumps to](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/covid-claims-further-28-000-jobs-as-qld-hits-18-year-unemployment-high-20200618-p553yg.html) 7.9 per cent, the highest since February 2002.

### June

**June 2:** A [coroner rules](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/health-minister-deeply-sorry-for-blackwater-scare-but-has-no-regrets-20200602-p54ylw.html) Nathan Turner, thought to be Australia’s youngest COVID-19 victim, did not have the virus after he [tests negative](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/calls-for-answers-and-apologies-after-central-queensland-covid-19-scare-20200602-p54yjw.html). Health authorities believe his initial test was a false-positive, prompting [backlash](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/coronavirus-victim-nathan-turner-tests-negative-queensland-health-confirms-20200601-p54yiu.html) from the small mining community. Queensland Health director-general John Wakefield [reveals his department](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/icus-were-braced-to-ventilate-enough-people-to-fill-suncorp-stadium-20200602-p54yqp.html) was planning for 20 per cent of the state, about 1 million people, to contract coronavirus during the first wave, initially predicted to last six months. About 50,000 people are predicted to get seriously ill and require ventilation in intensive care.

**June 6:** A Melbourne fruit picker [tests positive](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/melbourne-man-tests-positive-after-flying-to-brisbane-bundaberg-for-work-20200606-p5504v.html) for COVID-19 after flying to Brisbane and then on to Bundaberg.

**June 7:**[Tens of thousands of people pour into Brisbane’s CBD](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/link/follow-20170101-p5505u) for a Black Lives Matter protest.

Cleaning spray on the table at Crew in Burleigh Heads on May 16, 2020.Getty

**June 14**: Hundreds of activists defy health advice to[protest the indefinite detention](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/councillor-charged-after-hundreds-protest-indefinite-detention-20200614-p552cw.html) of asylum seekers being held at a Kangaroo Point hotel.

**June 15:** One-hundred people now allowed to [attend funerals.](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/more-people-allowed-at-queensland-funerals-from-tuesday-20200615-p552m0.html)

**June 16:** Palaszczuk [scraps the rule](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/palaszczuk-walks-back-from-original-border-benchmark-20200616-p55349.html) that other states must record 28 days of no community transmission for borders to open.

**June 17:** Laws are rushed through Parliament to[freeze the wages](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/wage-freeze-laws-to-be-rushed-through-parliament-this-week-20200615-p552oo.html) of hundreds of thousands of public servants for a year in a bid to save $500 million.

**June 18:** Aged care [lockdown lifts](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/nursing-home-lockdown-over-in-queensland-20200618-p553sw.html), with visitors allowed for the first time since March. Queensland to [start charging](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/queensland-to-charge-travellers-up-to-200-a-night-for-quarantine-20200618-p553qy.html) travellers for their mandatory hotel quarantine stays.

**June 20:** Up to [100 fans](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/stadiums-open-to-queensland-fans-at-weekend-trial-run-20200617-p553cy.html) are allowed inside sporting stadiums.

**June 25:** Miles admits the state [faced shortages](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/health-minister-admits-there-were-ppe-shortages-in-public-hospitals-20200625-p555z4.html) of personal protective equipment during the height of the pandemic after authorities had earlier assured there had been no supply issues.

**June 30**: Melbourne lockdown begins as Queensland announces it will [reopen its borders](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/queensland-will-reopen-its-borders-on-july-10-but-not-to-victorians-20200630-p557ij.html) on July 10, but not to Victorians.

Cardboard cut-outs of fans at the Gabba on June 13, 2020. Jono Searle/Getty

### July

**July 3:** House parties, nightclubs, casinos and concerts [are back](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/house-parties-nightclubs-and-concerts-are-back-but-will-be-different-20200630-p557ox.html). One-hundred people allowed to gather indoors and event attendance can swell to 10,000 people if venues have a COVID-safe plan.

**July 7:** The state government says plan to bring international students back into the state is [“almost finalised”](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/politics/queensland/plan-to-bring-foreign-students-back-to-queensland-almost-final-20200706-p559gm.html) (six months on, it has not happened).

**July 10:** Queensland’s border reopens to all but travellers from Victoria, where hundreds of new cases are being reported each day.

**July 14:** The NSW local government areas of [Liverpool and Campbelltown](https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/minogue-avoids-queensland-hotel-quarantine-as-premier-declares-nsw-hot-spots-20200714-p55btj.html) will be declared COVID-19 hotspots.

**July 24:** Queenslanders are told they must sit down at pubs and restaurants again.

**July 29:** All of greater Sydney declared a hotspot. A pair of travellers test positive for COVID-19 after travelling from Melbourne and avoiding mandatory quarantine. Queensland has gone three months without community transmission.

**July 30:**Olivia Muranga, Diana Lasu and Haja Timbo are accused of providing false information to border officials and are charged.

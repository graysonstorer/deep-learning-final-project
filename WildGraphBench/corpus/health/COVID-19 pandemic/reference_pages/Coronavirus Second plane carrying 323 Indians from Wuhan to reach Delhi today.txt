New Delhi,UPDATED:Feb 2, 2020 08:58 IST

A second Air India plane that left from New Delhi around 1.37 pm on Saturday to bring back Indian nationals is set to land in New Delhi on Sunday.

Of the 323, 104 are housed at Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) Chawla Camp and the remaining 220 are in Manesar, Haryana.

**"They are being effectively monitored,"** Government of India said.

They came to India on board another Air India flight which departed for the Chinese city from New Delhi around 1.37 pm to bring back Indian nationals. The plane reached Wuhan around 5.40 pm on Saturday.

advertisement

Earlier in the day, another set of 324 Indians were evacuated from China's Wuhan city reached Delhi on board Air India's jumbo B747 aircraft and were admitted at the two quarantine facilities set up by the Army and the ITBP, though none of them have tested positive for the novel coronavirus, officials said.

The plane, carrying 211 students, 110 working professionals and three minors, reached Delhi around 7.30 am, they said, adding that none of them have tested positive for the coronavirus as yet.

According to an ANI report, Government of India said that as of today 52,332 passengers from 326 flights were screened. Total 97 symptomatic travellers have been referred to isolation facilities and 98 samples were tested of which 97 found to be negative. Earlier, positive case was found in Kerala which is being monitored and is stable.

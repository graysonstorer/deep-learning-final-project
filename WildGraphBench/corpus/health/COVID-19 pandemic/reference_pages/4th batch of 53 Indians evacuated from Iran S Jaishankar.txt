Synopsis

This comes a day after over 230 Indians were brought back from Iran to New Delhi and quarantined at the Indian Army Wellness Centre in Jaisalmer, the third batch to be evacuated from that country.

![Image 6: 4](https://img.etimg.com/thumb/msid-74647533,width-300,height-225,imgsize-705948,resizemode-75/4.jpg)PTI

Indian nationals evacuated from Iran undergo a disinfectant process before being quarantined in Indian Army Wellness Facility Centre at Jaisalmer Military Station.

NEW DELHI: A fourth batch of 53 Indians returned to India from [Iran](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/topic/iran) on Monday, taking the total number of people evacuated from the coronavirus-hit country to 389.

This comes a day after over 230 Indians were brought back from Iran to New Delhi and quarantined at the [Indian](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/topic/indian) Army Wellness Centre in [Jaisalmer](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/topic/jaisalmer), the third batch to be evacuated from that country.

"Fourth batch of 53 Indians - 52 students and a teacher - has arrived from Tehran and Shiraz, Iran. With this, a total of 389 Indians have returned to India from Iran. Thank the efforts of the team @India_in_Iran and Iranian authorities," Jaishankar tweeted.

### Live Events

The first batch of 58 Indian pilgrims were brought back from Iran last Tuesday and the second group of 44 Indian pilgrim arrived from there on Friday.

Iran is one of the worst-affected countries by the [coronavirus](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/coronavirus) outbreak and the government has been working to bring back Indians stranded there. Over 700 people have died from the disease in Iran and nearly 14,000 cases have been detected.

Jaishankar had told Rajya Sabha last week that the government was focusing on evacuating Indians stranded in Iran and Italy as these countries are facing an "extreme situation".

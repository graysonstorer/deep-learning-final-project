Uncertain times call for innovative measures, and English singer-songwriter Dominic Harrison, who goes by the moniker Yungblud, is ahead of the curve.

The 22-year-old artist, who was set to kick off his North American tour at [both weekends of the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival in Indio in April](https://www.ocregister.com/2020/03/10/coachella-and-stagecoach-festivals-rescheduled-for-october/), has watched his concert dates drop off as they‚Äôre either canceled or postponed in the wake of the global spread of COVID-19.

As governments around the world mandate social distancing, Yungblud‚Äôs international tour dates have been postponed as well. In an effort to connect with his fans, Yungblud is one of the first major recording artists to announce [a live stream concert via his YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCd3Llj2WWJh8ItfYl32eJ4Q) at 7 a.m. PST Monday, March 16.

‚ÄúThe world is so weird right now, and it‚Äôs a really, really weird time of life,‚Äù he said via a phone chat over the weekend. ‚ÄúMagic just sort of happens in stages, and I had this idea since the opportunity to connect with my fans has been taken away from me because my shows are getting canceled left, right and center ‚Ä¶ I‚Äôm not going to just sit here in my house and twiddle my thumbs.‚Äù

Yungblud‚Äôs live stream will be broadcast from The Beehive Studios in Van Nuys, and rather than do a typical set, he‚Äôs opted to do it more like a late-night TV show where he‚Äôs the host.

‚ÄúWhen I posted the idea, I had a lot of friends who were stuck at home too that were like, ‚ÄòLet us get in on this‚Äô,‚Äù he said.

The show will include performances and special guests, including his friend and [fellow artist Machine Gun Kelly](https://www.ocregister.com/2019/06/13/machine-gun-kelly-gives-us-the-dirt-on-his-new-album-hotel-diablo-before-coming-to-southern-california/), actress and singer Bella Thorne and singer-songwriter and producer Oliver Tree.

‚ÄúWe‚Äôre just trying to spread a bit of positivity to a negative situation right now,‚Äù he said. ‚ÄúI think the world is so topsy-turvy and dark right now. We want to provide a bit of light, fire and distraction and some happiness, too, man.‚Äù

Though Yungblud was among the first to get a jump on announcing a major live stream concert, other local, regional, national and international acts are quickly getting on board.

Vancouver‚Äôs Celtic [punk rock band The Real McKenzies](https://www.facebook.com/therealmckenzies/) live-streamed its performance in an empty venue Friday night via Facebook when its stop at The Starlite Room in Edmonton, Canada had abruptly been canceled.

Fullerton-based Burger Records, which was forced to cancel its daylong Burgerama 5 fest in Los Angeles on March 14, announced it would live-stream a concert with indie bands Shadow Show and This Uni from Burger Records since the artists were already in town for the show.

Boston-based [Irish punk rock band Dropkick Murphy‚Äôs announced on Saturday](https://www.facebook.com/DropkickMurphys/) that it would be streaming its annual St. Patrick‚Äôs Day show live via YouTube, Instagram and Facebook starting at 4 p.m. PST.

‚ÄúFor the first time in 24 years, we are not playing on St. Patrick‚Äôs Day weekend,‚Äù the band shared via its social media pages. ‚ÄúThe current world situation is the only thing that would ever stop us from doing so.‚Äù

Orange County-based musicians Greta Valenti and Robin Davey, who are in local rock band Well Hung Heart and [blues-folk band Beaux Gris Gris & The Apocalypse](https://www.facebook.com/beauxgrisgris/), had to cancel spring tour dates in France for Beaux Gris Gris. That band also includes members who reside in the U.K., so, for now, they‚Äôve decided to upload previously recorded concerts and new content via its social media pages.

‚ÄúThis is going to hurt so many people,‚Äù Valenti said via a phone chat. ‚ÄúNo one can earn money right now. Everything is getting canceled and it‚Äôs not just the musicians that are being hurt ‚Äì it‚Äôs the crews, the venues, the promoters.‚Äù

In some ways, Valenti said she feels like this situation will actually bring people closer together since social distancing has led to smaller groups congregating or people just staying in their homes.

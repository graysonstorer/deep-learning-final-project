Steam now lets you buy your games in bitcoin
--------------------------------------------

### In case you still have a ton of bitcoin lying around

Valve and Bitpay have partnered to integray bitcoin payment into Steam, the tech company[announced](https://blog.bitpay.com/load-your-steam-wallet-using-bitcoin/). International customers can now use the virtual currency to purchase software off the platform.

"Valve reached out to us because they were looking for a fast, international payment method for Steam users in emerging gaming markets in countries like India, China, and Brazil," the bitcoin developer wrote in a post. "While more users are coming online in in these countries, traditional payment options like credit cards often aren't available."

The open-source,[occasionally controversial](http://www.polygon.com/2014/5/5/5677734/bitcoin-plex-isk-eve-online) digital payment system first hit the internet back in 2009. Its inventor, Satoshi Nakamoto, has attained notoriety as a mysterious, largely untraceable figure. We went in search of him in 2013; read[our findings](http://www.polygon.com/features/2013/9/4/4660780/bitcoin-satoshi-nakamoto) for more.

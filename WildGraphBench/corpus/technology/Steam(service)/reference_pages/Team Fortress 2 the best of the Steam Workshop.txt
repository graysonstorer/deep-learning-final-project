Team Fortress 2: the best of the Steam Workshop
===============================================

[Tom Senior](http://www.pcgamer.com/author/tomsenior/ "Posts by Tom Senior") at 04:27pm October 19 2011 

[Comments](http://www.pcgamer.com/2011/10/19/team-fortress-2-the-best-of-the-steam-workshop/#comments "Team Fortress 2: the best of the Steam Workshop Comments")45

![Image 14: Team Fortress 2 - The Steam Workshop](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-The-Steam-Workshop-610x239.jpg)
Among the many additions made by the recent Manniversary update for Team Fortress 2, the Steam Workshop may well turn out to be the most important. The Workshop is a hub that lets community item creators show off their creations so that they can be rated by other players. The best ones rise to the top, where they can be cherry-picked by Valve for inclusion in the game.

If you haven’t had chance to browse some of the community-made items vying for dominance, it’s well worth doing so. We’ve picked out some of our favourite items, including an umbrella-gun for the Spy, a beserk rocket launcher for the Soldier, and a couple of wonderfully mad Engineer packs.

[![Image 15](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-Mobile-Sentry-Gun2.jpg)](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-Mobile-Sentry-Gun2.jpg)

### The Mobile Engineer Pack

The [Mobile Sentry Gun](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=125) is deservedly the very highest rated item on the Steam Workshop right now. The model for the Engineer’s shoulder-mounted cannon is fantastic, but it also forms part of a larger pack that includes designs for [a number of different shoulder cannons](http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6169/6197131686_d1aa435cb0_b.jpg), and a [bionic hat](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=135) made up of infra-red vision equipment.

[![Image 16](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-The-Birds-Eye-Bomber1.jpg)](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-The-Birds-Eye-Bomber1.jpg)

### The Bird’s Eye Bomber

The physics of the [Bird’s Eye Bomber](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=230) don’t make any real-world sense, but it’s just the sort of mad contraption you could imagine the Soldier endorsing enthusiastically. The idea is that a secondary rocket can be fired downwards for easy rocket-jumping, and to bombard foes below as you fly overhead. Perfect for Soldiers obsessively seeking air superiority.

[![Image 17: Team Fortress 2 - the Aristocratic Assassin](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-the-Aristocratic-Assassin1.jpg)](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-the-Aristocratic-Assassin1.jpg)

### The Aristocratic Assassin Spy Umbrella

The best part of this inventive pack gives the Spy a gun hidden inside an umbrella. For some reason the vision of a Spy running around with an umbrella just works, and it gets even better when you discover that the Spy can open it up to break long falls. Watch [this video](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SDLhgLS4gww&feature=related) of a Spy doing just that. It’s like Mary Poppins. If Mary Poppins was French. And a trained killer.

### The Frankenstein Curios Engineer Pack

[![Image 18: Team Fortress 2 - The loboto-sentry 1](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-The-loboto-sentry-1.jpg)](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-The-loboto-sentry-1.jpg)

Nothing suggests mad science more effectively than a brain in a jar. The Frankenstein Curios pack for the engineer contains a [fantastic sentry gun powered by one such brain](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=50).

Items are submitted to the Steam Workshop without rules attached. When Valve adopt a new weapon, they decide how it will work in a way that hopefully won’t unbalance the game. That leaves the doors wide open for speculation on this one. What attributes would you give to a sentry gun that has a brain? There’s also a [brain-powered shotgun](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=43) and a [Mr. Freeze style dome helm](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=14) that makes the Engineer look genuinely quite menacing. See for yourself in this video of the pack in action.

[![Image 19: Team Fortress 2 - Spy voodoo dolls](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-Spy-voodoo-dolls1.jpg)](http://media.pcgamer.com/files/2011/10/Team-Fortress-2-Spy-voodoo-dolls1.jpg)

### Best accessories

These items should nicely fill the extra spare accessories slot that Valve added in the latest update. They wouldn’t necessarily have any effect on the game, but who’s pass up on a chance to carry around a pair of [Spy voodoo dolls](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=212)? There’s a [hilarious moustache for the Heavy](http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=324). We’re unsure whether it’s supposed to turn him into Stalin or Freddie Mercury, but damn, what a ‘tache.

Have you had a chance to browse the Steam Workshop? Got any favourite items of your own? Can you think of any good rule-sets for the items above? Let us know in the comments.

French consumer advocacy group [UFC Que Choisir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UFC_Que_Choisir) has filed suit against Valve -- alleging that its policies with Steam are problematic and even, in some cases, run afoul of the law.

The news comes via a Reddit post by user Silencement that [summarizes](https://www.reddit.com/r/Games/comments/3x871m/valve_sued_by_french_consumer_association/) UFC Que Choisir's own news story [[link here](http://www.quechoisir.org/telecom-multimedia/informatique/communique-plateforme-de-jeux-video-steam-l-ufc-que-choisir-assigne-la-societe-valve), French language] on its lawsuit. Here's that user's summary of the the organization's points:

*   _Steam's Subscriber Agreement explicitely forbids users to sell their games, despite the transfer of ownership of digital products/licenses being legal_
*   _Valve declines any responsibility in case they get hacked and users' personal info get stolen_
*   _Valve claims ownership on the rights of any user-created content uploaded on Steam_
*   _It is impossible to get the money on your Steam Wallet back if your account is closed/deleted/banned_
*   _Valve applies Luxembourg's consumer law regardless of the user's country_

This sort of consumer-focused lawsuit appears to be par for the course of the group; last year, UFC Que Choisir [sued](http://www.zdnet.com/article/google-facebook-twitter-face-lawsuit-over-illegible-incomprehensible-privacy-policies/) internet giants Twitter, Facebook, and Google over their privacy policies -- calling them "incomprehensible" and "illegible." Outside of the internet realm, the group also [filed](http://www.thelocal.fr/20141001/first-ever-class-action-suit-filed-in-france) France's first-ever class action lawsuit against rental property company Foncia,on behalf of renters.

Dota Workshop Opens
===================

 By [Katie Williams](http://www.gamespy.com/contact-us.html) | Jun 17, 2012

Additions to your wardrobe or your wallet, depending on how you see it.

[](http://pc.gamespy.com/pc/dota-2010/1225114p1.html# "Send to Facebook_like")[](http://pc.gamespy.com/pc/dota-2010/1225114p1.html# "Tweet")[](http://pc.gamespy.com/pc/dota-2010/1225114p1.html# "Send to Google_plusone")

![Image 4: Dota Workshop Guidelines and Resources, a banner featuring a blacksmith and his assistant working at a forge](http://pcmedia.gamespy.com/pc/image/object/083/083236/dotaworkshop.jpg)

 Hats! You know, I didn't think they'd ever take off, even despite my rabid collection of them in a certain hypercute Korean MMO a decade ago. And these days, no one ever _takes off_ their hat, so you might as well jump on board the trend-train. Steam's just launched the [Dota Workshop](http://dota2.com/workshop?l=english), allowing cosmetic customization for your [Dota 2](http://pc.gamespy.com/pc/dota-2010/index.html) heroes.

 So how does this work? If you have any 3D modelling skills (and hey, probably even if you don't), you can craft some of your own pretty [player-made Dota 2 accessories](http://au.pc.gamespy.com/pc/dota-2010/1224922p1.html) for heroes and upload them via the Workshop, to be voted on by the Dota 2 community and vetted by [Valve](http://www.gamespy.com/company/026/026417.html). Highly rated items will be purchasable in-game by other players, whose wallets will hopefully gladly cough up for your sweet sense of style. Valve will withhold 75% of said wallet-phlegm, and you'll receive the rest.

 The site links to models for existing heroes, so that you'll be able to mold your items to their curves and their personalities. And that's another thing: Valve will reportedly be vetting submissions heavily, so don't expect your immersion-breaking My Little Pony-themed item or anything vulgar to be accepted anytime soon. 

 My 3D modelling skills are near nil, but I know enough to be able to craft a cone, pattern it with a speckled MS Paint texture, and call it a party hat. I like to think of it as my contribution to the celebration of hopelessly stylish gamers everywhere. Will you be participating in the festivities?

Over 8,000 games were released on Steam in 2019, according to SteamSpy
======================================================================

![Image 96: Over 8,000 games were released on Steam in 2019, according to SteamSpy ](https://media.pcgamesinsider.biz/2018/5/88541/steamspy-r225x.png)

##### View more

*   [News](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/news/)
*   [Data & Research](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/data-and-research/)

##### Companies

*   [SteamSpy](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/company/19565/steamspy/)
*   [Valve](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/company/3587/valve/)

January 2nd, 2020 - 11:34am

By [Alex Calvin](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/staff/100199/alex-calvin/), Editor - PC Games Insider

The flow of new games coming to Steam did not slow down in 2019 with third-party data site SteamSpy reporting that over 8,000 titles were released during the year.

As always, SteamSpy isn't 100 per cent accurate so take all these figures with a pinch of salt, but [the site claims that 8,286 games launched on Valve's platform during 2019](https://steamspy.com/year/), with the median price of a new game clocking in at $4.79, a slight decline on 2018's $4.99. The average price of a new title, however, dipped from $8.87 to $7.50 during last year.

SteamSpy also reports that there are now 34,039 games now available on Steam, [an increase on the 27,397 that the site reported were up for sale in January 2018](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/news/68367/there-are-now-over-27000-games-on-steam/).

The median time that users spent playing a game rose to 13:29. Meanwhile, 137.6m titles launched in 2019 were bought during the year.

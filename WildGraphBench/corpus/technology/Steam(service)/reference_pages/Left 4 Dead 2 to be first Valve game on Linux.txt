'Left 4 Dead 2' to be first Valve game on Linux
===============================================

By [Tracey Lien](http://www.theverge.com/users/TraceyLien)on July 16, 2012 08:55 pm 61**Comments**

![Image 12: steam logo 375](http://cdn3.sbnation.com/entry_photo_images/4685670/steam_375_large_verge_medium_landscape.jpg)

0

[in Share](javascript:void(0);)

_Left 4 Dead 2_ will be the first Valve game optimized for Linux once the developer gets the Steam client onto Linux with full functionality.

In Valve's [debut Linux blog post](http://blogs.valvesoftware.com/linux/steamd-penguins/), the developer and publisher announced that it has been looking into the possibility of moving Steam and the Source game engine to Linux. Valve has formed a new team whose main purpose is to investigate the possibility of moving the Steam client and Left 4 Dead 2 over to the computer operating system Ubuntu.

The blog posts reads: "After successfully porting L4D2 to Ubuntu, interest grew within Valve and, as a result, the team and projects we were working on also grew. Currently, our focus is on the following projects:

▪ getting the Steam client onto Linux with full functionality

 ▪ optimizing a version of L4D2 running at a high frame rate with OpenGL

 ▪ porting additional Valve titles"

There is no word on when _Left 4 Dead_ and other ported Valve titles will be made available to Linux users. Readers can follow Valve's progress on their [blog](http://blogs.valvesoftware.com/linux/steamd-penguins/).

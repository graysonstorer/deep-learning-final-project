By [Emily Davies](https://www.washingtonpost.com/people/emily-davies/)

President Donald Trump on Thursday began his crackdown on telework, a sweeping decree that [officials estimate](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/OMB-Report-to-Congress-on-Telework-and-Real-Property.pdf) could impact more than 1 million federal workers.

The White House’s Office of Personnel Management in a memo directed heads of federal agencies to revise their telework policies by 5 p.m. Friday and gave employees roughly 30 days from then to comply — panicking workers who rely on the flexibility for child care, affordable housing and to save time on long commutes.

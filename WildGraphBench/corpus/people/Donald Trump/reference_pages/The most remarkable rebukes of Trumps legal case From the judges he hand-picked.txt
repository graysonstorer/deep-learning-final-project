The most remarkable rebukes of Trump’s legal case: From the judges he hand-picked
=================================================================================

At least nine Trump-appointed judges or Supreme Court short-listers have declined to bolster his claims of election fraud. None have ruled in his favor.

December 14, 2020 More than**4 years ago**

Analysis by [Aaron Blake](https://www.washingtonpost.com/people/aaron-blake/)

For much of his four years in office, President Trump has hailed the appointment of a historically large number of judges as a conservative, political accomplishment. Before appointing Amy Coney Barrett to the Supreme Court, he argued that she needed to be confirmed before Election Day so she could [help prevent the election from being stolen](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-marine-one-departure-092220/). And shortly after Election Day, Trump surrogate Harmeet Dhillon made it abundantly clear that the campaign expected Barrett and Trump’s other appointees to be [_on the team_](https://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/524727-trump-surrogate-on-election-legal-challenges-hopefully-amy-coney-barrett).
